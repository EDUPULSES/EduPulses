Evolution of Atomic Models

Thomson's Model (Plum Pudding)

Atom is a sphere of positive charge with electrons embedded in it like dry fruits in a pudding or seeds in a watermelon. Failed: Could not explain Rutherford's experiment.

Rutherford's Model (Nuclear Model)

Famous Gold Foil Experiment (Alpha scattering).
1. Most space is empty.
2. Positive charge (Nucleus) is very small and heavy in center.
3. Electrons revolve around nucleus.

Bohr's Model

Electrons revolve in discrete orbits (Shells: K, L, M, N). While revolving in these orbits, they do not radiate energy.

Electronic Configuration

Rules:

  • 1. Max electrons in a shell = $2n^2$ (K=2, L=8, M=18).
  • 2. Outermost shell cannot hold more than 8 electrons.
  • 3. Shells are filled step-wise.

Example: Sodium (Na)

Isotopes

Atoms of same element, same Atomic Number, different Mass Number.

Ex: Carbon-12 & Carbon-14.
Applications: U-235 (Nuclear fuel), Co-60 (Cancer treatment).

Isobars

Atoms of different elements, different Atomic Number, same Mass Number.

Ex: Calcium (Z=20) and Argon (Z=18) both have Mass=40.
