Is Matter Around Us Pure?

Purity in Nature

Most matter around us exists as mixtures of two or more pure components. For a scientist, "Pure" means all constituent particles are the same in their chemical nature.

What is a Mixture?

Constitutes by more than one kind of pure form of matter.

  • Homogeneous: Uniform composition (Salt water).
  • Heterogeneous: Non-uniform composition (Sand and salt).

What is a Solution?

A homogeneous mixture of two or more substances.

Solvent Component in larger amount (dissolves other).
Solute Component in smaller amount (gets dissolved).

Separating Components

Technique 01

Evaporation

To separate volatile component (solvent) from non-volatile solute.
Ex: Dye from ink.

Technique 02

Centrifugation

Denser particles forced to bottom, lighter stay at top when spun rapidly.
Ex: Cream from milk.

Technique 03

Sublimation

To separate sublimable volatile component from non-sublimable impurity.
Ex: Ammonium chloride & Salt.

Technique 04

Chromatography

Separation of solutes that dissolve in the same solvent.
Ex: Pigments from flower petals.

Technique 05

Distillation

For two miscible liquids with sufficient difference in boiling points.
Ex: Acetone and water.

Technique 06

Crystallization

To obtain pure solid from solution. Better than evaporation as it prevents decomposition.
Ex: Purification of salt.

Pure Substances

Elements

Basic form of matter that cannot be broken down (Metals, Non-metals, Metalloids). Ex: Gold, Oxygen.

Compounds

Composed of two or more elements combined chemically in a fixed proportion. Properties differ from constituents. Ex: Water ($H_2O$).
