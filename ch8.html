Force & Laws of Motion

Newton's Laws

Understanding why things move. Force is the push or pull that changes the state of rest or motion of a body.

Balanced Forces

If the resultant of all forces acting on a body is zero, the forces are balanced. They cannot change the state of motion, but can change shape (squeezing a balloon).

Unbalanced Forces

If the resultant force is not zero. These forces produce a change in the state of rest or motion. This causes acceleration.

1

First Law of Motion (Inertia)

"An object remains in a state of rest or of uniform motion in a straight line unless compelled to change that state by an applied force."

Inertia

The natural tendency of objects to resist a change in their state of motion. Mass is the measure of inertia. Heavier object = More Inertia.

Bus starts suddenly: Passengers fall backward (Feet move with bus, upper body stays at rest).
Bus stops suddenly: Passengers fall forward (Feet stop, upper body continues motion).
2

Second Law of Motion

"The rate of change of momentum of an object is proportional to the applied unbalanced force in the direction of force."

$$ F = m \times a $$

Where F is Force, m is Mass, and a is Acceleration.
Unit of Force: Newton (N).

Real Life Application

Catching a ball: A cricketer pulls his hands back while catching a fast ball. This increases the time for the velocity to drop to zero, reducing acceleration and thus reducing the Force impact on hands.

3

Third Law of Motion

"To every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction."

  • These two forces always act on different objects.
  • Rocket: Gases pushed down (Action) -> Rocket pushed up (Reaction).
  • Walking: We push ground back -> Ground pushes us forward.
  • Gun Recoil: Bullet goes forward -> Gun kicks backward.

Law of Conservation of Momentum

In an isolated system (no external force), the total momentum remains conserved.

$$ m_1u_1 + m_2u_2 = m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 $$

Total momentum before collision = Total momentum after collision
