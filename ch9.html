Universal Gravity

Why do apples fall? Why do planets orbit the sun? The answer lies in the invisible force that binds the universe together.

Universal Law of Gravitation

Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force which is proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

$$ F = G \frac{M \times m}{d^2} $$
G: Universal Constant ($6.67 \times 10^{-11} Nm^2/kg^2$)
M, m: Masses of objects
d: Distance between centers

Free Fall

When an object falls towards the earth under the influence of gravitational force alone.

Acceleration due to gravity (g) = $9.8 m/s^2$.
It is independent of the mass of the object (Feather and Hammer fall at same rate in vacuum).

Thrust and Pressure

  • Thrust: The force acting on an object perpendicular to the surface.
  • Pressure: The thrust per unit area. ($P = \frac{Thrust}{Area}$)
  • Unit: Pascal (Pa).

Why sharp knives cut better?

Sharp edge = Very small Area. For same Force, Pressure becomes very large ($P \propto 1/A$), making it easy to cut.

Archimedes' Principle

"When a body is immersed fully or partially in a fluid, it experiences an upward force (Buoyancy) that is equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by it."

Applications: Designing ships, submarines, lactometers (milk purity), hydrometers.
Float vs Sink: Density < Water $\to$ Float. Density > Water $\to$ Sink.
