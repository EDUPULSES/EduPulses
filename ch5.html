The Fundamental Unit of Life

The Cell

All living organisms are made up of microscopic units called cells. It is the structural and functional unit of life. Discovered by Robert Hooke in 1665.

1665
Robert Hooke
Discovered cells in cork slice.
1674
Leeuwenhoek

1831
Robert Brown
Discovered the Nucleus.

Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic

Feature Prokaryotic (Bacteria) Eukaryotic (Plant/Animal)
Size Small (1-10 μm) Large (5-100 μm)
Nucleus Absent (Nucleoid) Present (Well defined)
Organelles Membrane-bound absent Present (Mitochondria, etc.)

Plasma Membrane

The outermost covering of the cell that separates the contents of the cell from its external environment.

  • • Selectively Permeable: Allows entry/exit of specific materials.
  • • Diffusion: Movement of gases (CO2, O2) from high to low concentration.
  • • Osmosis: Movement of water from high water concentration to low through a semi-permeable membrane.
Hypotonic Solution

Medium has more water. Cell gains water & swells.

Isotonic Solution

Same water concentration. No net change.

Hypertonic Solution

Medium has less water. Cell loses water & shrinks.

Cell Organelles

Nucleus

The Brain of the cell. Contains Chromosomes (DNA + Protein). Controls all cellular activities.

Mitochondria

Powerhouse of the cell. Generates energy in the form of ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate). Has its own DNA.

Lysosomes

Suicide Bags. Contain powerful digestive enzymes. If cell gets damaged, lysosome bursts and digests its own cell.

Plastids (Plants Only)

Chloroplasts (Green - Photosynthesis), Chromoplasts (Colored), Leucoplasts (White - Storage).

Vacuoles

Storage sacs. Small in animal cells, very large in plant cells (50-90% volume) providing turgidity.

Golgi Apparatus

Storage, modification, and packaging of products in vesicles. Formation of lysosomes.
