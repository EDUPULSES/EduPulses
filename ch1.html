Matter in Our Surroundings

The Nature of Matter

Everything in this universe is made up of material which scientists have named "matter". The air we breathe, the food we eat, stones, clouds, stars, plants and animals.

01 Physical Nature of Matter

Particulate Nature

Matter is made up of particles. It is not continuous like a block of wood, but rather like sand. These particles are extremely smallâ€”beyond our imagination.

Characteristics of Particles

  • They have space between them (Salt dissolving in water).
  • They are continuously moving (Incense stick aroma).
  • They attract each other (Force of attraction).

02 States of Matter

The Solid State

Solids have a definite shape, distinct boundaries, and fixed volumes. They have negligible compressibility. Solids may break under force but it is difficult to change their shape, so they are rigid.

Exception: Sponge We can compress a sponge, yet it is a solid because it has minute holes where air is trapped. Pressing it expels air.
Exception: Rubber Band Changes shape under force but regains it. Excessive force breaks it.
The Liquid State

Liquids have no fixed shape but have a fixed volume. They take up the shape of the container. Liquids flow and change shape, so they are not rigid but can be called fluid.

Diffusion: Solids, liquids, and gases can diffuse into liquids. The rate of diffusion of liquids is higher than solids.

The Gaseous State

Gases are highly compressible compared to solids and liquids. The LPG cylinder in your home or oxygen supplied to hospitals is compressed gas.

  • Due to high speed of particles and large space, gases show the property of diffusing very fast.
  • Particles hit the walls of the container, exerting pressure.

Latent Heat

The heat energy which has to be supplied to change the state of a substance is called latent heat. It does not raise the temperature but is used up in overcoming the forces of attraction between particles.

04 Evaporation

A surface phenomenon where liquid changes into vapor at any temperature below its boiling point.

Factors Affecting Evaporation:

Surface Area
Increases with increase in surface area. (Spreading clothes to dry).
Temperature
Increases with increase in temperature. (More kinetic energy).
Humidity
Decreases with increase in humidity. (Air already holds water).
Wind Speed
Increases with wind speed. (Particles move away).
Cooling Effect: Evaporation causes cooling because particles absorb energy from surroundings.
