The Building Blocks

From the ancient Indian philosopher Maharishi Kanad to modern chemistry. Understanding the smallest particles of matter.

Law of Conservation of Mass

Lavoisier (1774)

"Mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction."

Mass of Reactants = Mass of Products

Law of Constant Proportions

Proust (1779)

"In a chemical substance the elements are always present in definite proportions by mass."

Water ($H_2O$) is always 1:8 by mass (H:O).

Dalton's Atomic Theory

  • 1. All matter is made of tiny particles called atoms.
  • 2. Atoms are indivisible particles, which cannot be created or destroyed.
  • 3. Atoms of a given element are identical in mass and chemical properties.
  • 4. Atoms of different elements have different masses and properties.
  • 5. Atoms combine in the ratio of small whole numbers to form compounds.

What is an Atom?

The smallest particle of an element that retains its chemical properties. Radius is measured in nanometers ($10^{-9} m$).

Atomic Mass Unit (u)

Mass of one atom of an element is very small. Scientists use a relative scale.

1 unit = $\frac{1}{12}$ mass of one Carbon-12 atom.

The Mole Concept

6.022 × 1023
Avogadro Constant ($N_0$)

1 Mole of anything = $6.022 \times 10^{23}$ particles of that thing.

Formula 1
$$ n = \frac{m}{M} $$
n = Moles, m = Given Mass, M = Molar Mass
Formula 2
$$ n = \frac{N}{N_0} $$
N = Given Particles, $N_0$ = Avogadro No.
